Q: What do I need?

We love when our customers provide us with a PDF of what it is they are wanting to print. PDF's contain the document as-is and you are sure of ending up with exactly what you want.

Word documents should be treated as a last resort as they contain a lot of information which is not guaranteed to display exactly the same on every machine. So if you want your document to print as you imagined it would, convert it to a PDF first.

Q: What colour settings should my document be set in?

Your document should be set in the CMYK colour setting. This is the ensure the colours look almost the same as they did on your computer.

If your document is set in RGB mode, some of the colours may turn out much different than what was thought.

Q: What file formats do you print?
Here is a list of our most used file formats:
  • Portable Document File (.pdf)
  • Microsoft Word (.doc or .docx)
  • JPEG (.jpeg or .jpg)
  • Portable Network Graphic (.png)
  • Scalable Vector Graphic (.svg)
Visit us in store or send us an e-mail if your format isn't listed here!
